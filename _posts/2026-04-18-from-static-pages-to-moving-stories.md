There is a quiet shift that happens when you move from consuming content to creating it. For me, that shift began sometime in 2024 not with a grand plan, but with a single decision: **to show up**.

It started with <a href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/realabernard" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">LinkedIn</a>. I created my account, unsure of what direction it would take, but certain that I wanted to exist in the space where ideas are shared, challenged, and refined. From there, things began to expand organically. One platform led to another: <a href="https://www.facebook.com/andy.molecular.biologist/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Facebook</a>, <a href="https://www.instagram.com/andy.molecular.biologist" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Instagram</a>, <a href="https://www.youtube.com/@TMB_Room" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">YouTube</a>, <a href="https://github.com/BernardAndy" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">GitHub</a>, <a href="https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Andy-Bernard" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">ResearchGate</a>, <a href="https://medium.com/@realabernard" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Medium</a>, <a href="https://www.threads.net/@andy.molecular.biologist" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Threads</a>, and beyond.

What began as isolated accounts gradually became a connected ecosystem each platform linked, each serving a purpose, each contributing to a growing digital presence.

At the center of it all, however, has been <a href="https://www.facebook.com/andy.molecular.biologist/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong>Facebook</strong></a>. That's where I've been most active. It's where I test ideas, share thoughts, and observe how people engage with scientific content when it is presented in a simple, relatable way. My focus has remained consistent: molecular biology, genomics, and the broader conversations that surround science and life.

But content creation is not just about writing.

That realization led me to my current phase arguably the most challenging one yet.

## The Struggle With Motion

While text came naturally, video did not.

Animation, in particular, felt like a different world entirely. The kind of scientific videos I envision clear, visual, explanatory require more than just knowledge. They demand structure, timing, and movement. And movement, I've come to realize, is a skill on its own.

So, I started simple.

Instead of waiting until I could create perfect animations, I chose a more practical entry point: **working with static images**.

Using [InShot](https://inshot.com/) (with a subscription to unlock its full capabilities), I began experimenting:
* Importing images.
* Cutting them into parts.
* Moving them across the screen.
* Aligning them with narration.
* Creating the illusion of motion where there was none.

At first glance, it may seem basic and it is. But it's also foundational.

> Because behind every polished animation is an understanding of how elements move, how attention shifts, and how visuals can guide understanding.

## Learning by Doing

My approach is straightforward:

1. Create one video.
2. Then create another.
3. Then another.

No pressure for perfection. Just progression.

Each attempt teaches something new: how to position objects, how to transition between scenes, how to maintain viewer attention. Over time, these small improvements compound. What feels difficult today begins to feel natural tomorrow.

Right now, I'm not trying to compete with advanced animation studios. I'm building a skill from the ground up learning how to manipulate visual elements, how to turn still images into storytelling tools.

And eventually, I'll evolve:
* More styles.
* Better techniques.
* More refined storytelling.

But for now, this is the phase I'm in, and I'm embracing it fully.

## Why This Matters

This journey is not just about content creation. It's about **communication**.

Science, especially fields like molecular biology, is often locked behind complex language and static diagrams. My goal is to break that barrier to translate scientific ideas into formats that are easier to see, understand, and engage with.

And video is a powerful medium for that.

## A Living Portfolio

As part of this journey, I'm documenting everything not just on social platforms, but also here on <a href="https://github.com/BernardAndy" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong>GitHub</strong></a> as part of my portfolio.

Not because it's perfect.
**But because it's real.**

This is the beginning of something. A transition from static knowledge to dynamic storytelling. From written explanations to visual experiences.

And like any meaningful process, it starts small.
